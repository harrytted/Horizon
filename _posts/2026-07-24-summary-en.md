This milestone highlights the rising prominence of Chinese mathematicians on the global stage and underscores the depth of talent in China's mathematical community. Deng Yu was recognized for contributions to partial differential equations, including a rigorous derivation of the Boltzmann equation from hard-sphere dynamics, while Wang Hong was honored for advances in harmonic analysis and geometric measure theory, such as applying multiscale and decoupling techniques to the local smoothing conjecture for the wave equation.

## [Astronomers may have found the first exomoon](https://www.eso.org/public/news/eso2610/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Astronomers have announced the potential discovery of the first exomoon, designated CD-35 2722 b I, orbiting a brown dwarf in a binary system. The finding, if confirmed, would mark the first detection of a moon beyond our solar system. This discovery challenges current classification schemes for planets and moons, as the brown dwarf and its candidate moon are similar in size. It also demonstrates the growing capability of astronomers to detect smaller, more elusive celestial bodies, opening new avenues for studying planetary system formation and habitability. The candidate exomoon orbits a brown dwarf about 470 light-years away in the binary system CD-35 2722. The brown dwarf has a mass roughly 50 times that of Jupiter, while the exomoon is estimated to be about 0.5 Jupiter masses, making them closer in size than typical planet-moon pairs.

hackernews · MarcoDewey · Jul 23, 14:02 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49021783)

**Background**: An exomoon is a natural satellite that orbits an exoplanet or other non-stellar extrasolar body. Exomoons are notoriously difficult to detect with current technology, and no confirmed detections exist to date. Brown dwarfs are substellar objects with masses between giant planets and low-mass stars, insufficient to sustain hydrogen fusion but capable of deuterium fusion. This candidate exomoon was detected using transit timing variations, a method that looks for subtle changes in the timing of transits caused by the gravitational pull of a companion.

## [PyPI Blocks Uploads to Releases Older Than 14 Days](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/23/seth-larson/#atom-everything) ⭐️ 8.0/10

PyPI now rejects new file uploads to any release older than 14 days, a change implemented on July 22, 2026, to prevent poisoned old stable releases via compromised tokens. This is a significant supply-chain security hardening for the Python ecosystem, closing a loophole where attackers could inject malicious files into old, trusted releases after stealing a publishing token. The restriction applies to all projects on PyPI and was added via a pull request to the Warehouse codebase. As stated by Seth Larson, the attack vector has not been observed in the wild yet, but no technical barrier existed previously.

rss · Simon Willison · Jul 23, 04:50

**Background**: PyPI (Python Package Index) is the official repository for Python packages. Publishing tokens or API tokens are used to authenticate uploads, especially in CI/CD workflows. If a token is compromised, an attacker could upload a malicious file to an existing old release, which users trust. This change prevents that by allowing new files only within 14 days of a release's initial publication.
